All figures **ZAR** unless noted. USD at **~R18.5/$** (illustrative). Built bottom-up from
`01-financial-model.md` (prime / upper-market strategy).

## 1. Capital requirement (bottom-up)

The prime model burns cash only until EBITDA turns positive in **Y4**. The **cumulative cash
low-point is ≈ −R16.8m at the end of Y3**; with a ~R3–4m working-capital + contingency buffer,
the **total capital to reach self-sustaining is ≈ R20m (~$1.1m)** in the base case.

| | Conservative | Base | Aggressive |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Cumulative cash low-point | ~R17.8m | **~R16.8m** | ~R15.6m |
| + buffer | R3m | R3m | R3m |
| **Total capital to breakeven** | **~R20.8m** | **~R19.8m** | **~R18.6m** |

### Recommended raise — a single seed (low dilution)

Because the prime model reaches breakeven on ~R20m, **one well-sized seed round can fund the
whole journey to profitability** — no forced Series A, minimal dilution.

| Round | Timing | Amount | Use of funds | Milestone |
|---|---|---:|---|---|
| **Seed** | Now | **R20–25m (~$1.1–1.4m)** | CT-prime launch: lean concierge team (6→13), ooba + panel-conveyancer + WhatsApp integrations, brand in prime suburbs, first ~600 deals | EBITDA-positive (Y4), ~R26m revenue run-rate |
| **Series A** | *Optional* | R30–50m | Only if accelerating national-prime / adjacent segments faster than organic cash allows | Multi-metro prime leadership |

This is **well inside** the African seed band (~$1.6m avg) and leaves the Series A as a *growth
option*, not a survival necessity — a strong negotiating position.

## 2. Valuation

### a) Revenue-multiple (comps)

Global proptech ~**8.8× EV/revenue** (Finro 2025); discount to **~4–6×** for SA early-stage.
The prime model's **23% Y5 EBITDA margin** supports the upper half of that range (quality of
earnings), and could alternatively be valued on an EBITDA multiple as profitability matures.

| | Conservative | Base | Aggressive |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Y5 revenue | R27.4m | **R47.6m** | R85.0m |
| × 4–6× | R110–164m | **R190–285m** | R340–510m |

### b) VC method (work back from an exit)

- Base Y5 revenue **R47.6m at ~23% EBITDA margin** → target enterprise value **~R240m**
  (≈5× revenue) in ~5 years; aggressive **~R420–500m**.
- A seed investor at **~R45–55m pre-money**, holding to a ~R240m base outcome, models a
  **~3–5× gross return** — with the asymmetry (and most of the return) in the aggressive case
  and in the optionality to broaden beyond prime.

### c) Why pre-money holds despite lower top-line

A smaller, **profitable, capital-efficient** business with **higher margins and lower execution
risk** is not worth less than a bigger loss-making one — often the reverse to disciplined
investors. We keep seed pre-money at **~R45–55m**, justified by the live product, the deployed
dashboard, the defensible 0%-commission wedge, and a **clear path to profit on a single round.**

## 3. The Purplebricks guardrail — and why prime sidesteps it

Purplebricks (AIM IPO £240m → peak >£1bn → **sold for £1 in 2023**) was destroyed by
marketing-led CAC and over-expansion. **Prime focus is the structural antidote:** demand in
prime suburbs is **referral- and reputation-driven**, so marketing stays **~12% of revenue**
(not the blitz-scale spend that sank Purplebricks), and the concierge model keeps the team
small. We grow into adjacent segments **from profitability, on our own cash** — by choice, not
necessity.
