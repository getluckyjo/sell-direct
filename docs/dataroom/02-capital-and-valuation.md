# Sold Direct — Capital Requirement & Valuation

All figures **ZAR** unless noted. USD conversions at **~R18.5/$** (illustrative).
Built bottom-up from the model in `01-financial-model.md`.

## 1. Capital requirement (bottom-up)

The base case burns cash until EBITDA turns positive in Y5. The **cumulative cash low-point
is ≈ −R51.4m at the end of Y4**; with a working-capital + contingency buffer of ~R5m, the
**total capital to reach self-sustaining is ≈ R56m (~$3.0m)**.

| | Conservative | Base | Aggressive |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Cumulative cash low-point | ~R38m | **~R51m** | ~R56m |
| + buffer/working capital | ~R4m | ~R5m | ~R6m |
| **Total capital to breakeven** | **~R42m** | **~R56m** | **~R62m** |

### Recommended raise structure (staged — don't raise it all at once)

| Round | Timing | Amount | Use of funds | Milestone it buys |
|---|---|---:|---|---|
| **Seed** | Now | **R15–20m (~$0.8–1.1m)** | Cape Town launch: core team (8), WhatsApp + ooba + panel-conveyancer integrations live, first ~600 registered deals | Prove the loop end-to-end with real revenue per deal; ~R6m run-rate |
| **Series A** | ~18–24 months | **R35–45m (~$1.9–2.4m)** | Western Cape → national expansion, scale ops + partnerships, marketing engine | Reach base-case breakeven (Y5) without further dilution |
| **Total** | | **~R55–60m (~$3.0–3.2m)** | | Self-sustaining, EBITDA-positive |

This maps cleanly to African norms (seed ~$1.6m avg; Series A $3–10m). **Raising the seed
only against the Cape Town milestone minimises dilution** — the Series A is priced off proven
unit economics, not a forecast.

## 2. Valuation

Three lenses; all point to a **seed pre-money in the R40–55m range** and a **Year-5 enterprise
value of ~R300–460m (base)**.

### a) Revenue-multiple (comps)

Global proptech trades at **~8.8× EV/revenue** (Finro 2025). For an SA early-stage company we
**discount to ~4–6×** (smaller market, earlier stage, thinner comps).

| | Conservative | Base | Aggressive |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Y5 revenue | R27m | **R76.5m** | R140m |
| × 4–6× | R108–162m | **R306–459m** | R560–840m |

### b) VC method (work back from an exit)

- Base Y5 revenue **R76.5m**, EBITDA-positive and growing → target enterprise value
  **~R380m** (≈5× revenue) in ~5 years; aggressive case **R700m–1.1bn**.
- A seed investor entering at **~R45m pre-money** and holding (with dilution) to a ~R380m
  outcome models a **~4–6× gross return** on the base case, materially more on the upside —
  in range for SA venture, with the asymmetry sitting in the aggressive scenario.

### c) Stage benchmarks (sanity)

- African seed round avg **~$1.6m (~R30m)**; SA seed pre-money for a company with a **live
  product + first revenue** commonly **R40–70m**. Our **R45–55m pre-money** sits mid-range and
  is supported by the working WhatsApp product, the deployed dashboard, and a defensible
  0%-commission wedge.

### Suggested cap-table framing (illustrative)

| Round | Pre-money | Raise | Post-money | New-investor stake |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Seed | R45m | R18m | R63m | ~29% |
| Series A | ~R150m | R40m | R190m | ~21% |

## 3. The Purplebricks guardrail

The canonical commission-disruptor comp, **Purplebricks**, IPO'd at £240m, peaked above £1bn,
and **sold for £1 in 2023** — destroyed by marketing-led CAC and over-expansion. The model
reflects the lesson directly:

- **CAC discipline is the #1 risk** and the #1 sensitivity. Sold Direct's structural advantage
  is that WhatsApp service/utility messaging is **free in the 24-hour window** and portal
  syndication + referral virality pull effective CAC **below** paid-search benchmarks.
- The base case keeps marketing at **~21% of revenue (Y5)** and **does not** assume the
  blitz-scale spend that sank Purplebricks. The aggressive case spends more — and is explicitly
  labelled higher-risk.

## 4. What to confirm before circulating to investors

The numbers are **model-grade**, not yet **audit-grade**. Before sending out:
1. Re-confirm the **0.5% referral share** and the **conveyancing panel %** with ooba and a
   panel conveyancer (these are the two biggest revenue inputs and both are Medium/Low rel.).
2. Pull **Western Cape / Cape Town transfer counts** from Lightstone/Deeds Office to firm up
   the addressable-volume ramp.
3. Pin **SA-specific CAC** with a small paid pilot — replace the global proxy.
4. Validate **salary** lines (ops/sales) against live SA benchmarks.

See `00-assumptions.md §F` for the full gap list. Every gap has a clear owner action; none
changes the **shape** of the model, only the precision of individual lines.
