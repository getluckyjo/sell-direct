## 1. Capital requirement (bottom-up)

The model turns **EBITDA-positive in Y3** with a **cumulative cash low-point ≈ −R3.3m (Y2)** — the
Year-2 in-house origination uplift *improves* the cash trough versus taper-only. With a buffer, the
**total capital to reach self-sustaining is ≈ R6.3m (~$0.3m)** in the base case — still funded
largely from gross margin, not the raise.

| | Conservative | Base | Aggressive |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Cumulative cash low-point | ~R3.9m | **~R3.3m** | ~R2.5m |
| + buffer | R3m | R3m | R3m |
| **Total capital to breakeven** | **~R6.9m** | **~R6.3m** | **~R5.5m** |

### Recommended raise — a single seed to fund the growth push

The modelled *survival* need is only ~R6.3m, but raise a **single ~R15m seed** to **front-load the
ATL marketing**, **stand up in-house origination** (bank agreements + FAIS/FSP accreditation), and
de-risk the deal ramp — most of the round is offence, not survival.

| Round | Timing | Amount | Use of funds | Milestone |
|---|---|---:|---|---|
| **Seed** | Now | **R15m (~$0.8m)** | CT-prime launch + first brand campaign, AI-augmented team, ooba + panel-conveyancer + WhatsApp integrations, **secure bank aggregation agreements for Y2 in-house origination**, secure the bank sponsor | EBITDA-positive (Y3), brand established in prime CT, origination in-house |
| **Series A** | *Optional, growth* | R50m | Pour fuel on a proven ATL→deals engine; national upper-market rollout | Multi-metro leadership |

R15m is **well inside** the African seed band (~$1.6m avg). Because the business breaks even on a
fraction of it, **almost all of the round is deployable on growth, not survival** — the strongest
position for valuation and terms.

## 2. Valuation

### a) Revenue-multiple (comps)

Global proptech ~**8.8× EV/revenue** (Finro 2025); discount to **~4–6×** for SA early-stage.
The model pairs high growth with a **~39% Y5 EBITDA margin** — the Year-2 in-house origination
restores margin above the taper-only ~30%, while the broader upper-market pool keeps the volume
de-risked.

| | Conservative | Base | Aggressive |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Y5 revenue | R117.0m | **R188.9m** | R337.4m |
| × 4–6× | R468–702m | **R756m–1.13bn** | R1.35–2.02bn |

### b) VC method (work back from an exit)

- Base Y5 revenue **R188.9m at ~39% EBITDA margin** → target enterprise value **~R945m**
  (5× revenue) in ~5 years; aggressive **~R1.7bn**.
- A seed investor at **~R50m pre-money**, holding (with dilution) to the base outcome, models a
  **high-multiple return** — the capital-light profile drives it. **Temper this in the pitch:**
  lead with the defensible *revenue* line and the modest ~8% market share, not the headline margin.

### c) Why the pre-money is well-supported

A **profitable, capital-efficient** business that reaches breakeven on ~R6.3m is a strong story for
disciplined investors. We set seed pre-money at **~R50m**, justified by the live product, the
deployed dashboard, the defensible monetisation wedge (now including **owned origination
economics**), the broad addressable segment, and a **clear path to profit on a single small round.**

## 4. The Purplebricks guardrail — and why the taper sidesteps it

Purplebricks (AIM IPO £240m → peak >£1bn → **sold for £1 in 2023**) was destroyed by marketing-led
CAC and over-expansion. **Landing at prime is the structural antidote:** prime demand is referral-
and reputation-driven (lowest CAC), so we **earn brand trust before broadening down-market**, and
the AI-augmented concierge model keeps the team small. We taper into adjacent segments **from a
position of brand strength**, not a blitz-scale land-grab.
