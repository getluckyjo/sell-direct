## 5. Stress test & positioning (read this)

**The base case implies ~21% of the national prime market by Y5.** That is the number an investor
will probe. It moves inversely with the prime-pool estimate:

| Prime pool | Base Y5 share |
|---|---|
| 10,000 | **29%** |
| 14,000 (base) | **21%** |
| 20,000 | **15%** |

**Positioning to make it credible:**
- Frame the SOM as a **share of the prime niche**, not the whole market. As a % of *all* SA
  transfers the same plan is only **~1.2%** — but that framing hides the real (harder) ask, so
  don't lean on it.
- The model only works as the **category-defining prime channel** — the *only* 0%-commission,
  WhatsApp-native, concierge + ecosystem player. ~20% of a focused niche is achievable for a
  brand leader; ~20% of the whole market is not. **Precision of positioning is the make-or-break.**
- **De-risk the central assumption:** the prime-pool count (14k) and the ATL→deals conversion. Run
  a Cape Town prime pilot to prove both before scaling the R50m/yr marketing.
- **Honest downside:** if we win only ~10% of prime, that's still ~1,400 Y5 deals ≈ R100m revenue —
  the conservative scenario. The model is robust at half the base share.

## 6. Consumer value — commission saved

In South Africa the **seller** carries the agent commission, so the hard saving accrues to sellers;
buyers benefit via free bond pre-qualification, no agent mark-up, and a faster, transparent process.

| | Y1 | Y3 | Y5 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Saving per sale (vs 6% + VAT) | ~R415k | ~R428k | **~R442k** |
| Annual consumer saving | R20.5m | R278m | **R1.28bn** |
| Cumulative saving | R20.5m | R392m | **R2.33bn** |

> **By Year 5, Sold Direct saves South African sellers ~R1.3bn/year in commission — ~R2.3bn
> cumulatively over five years.** This is the consumer-value headline and the regulatory/PR
> goodwill story: we transfer ~R24bn/yr of industry inefficiency back to homeowners, one prime
> deal at a time.

*(Figures use a 6% + VAT benchmark and the base-case deal ramp; editable on the Market Sizing tab.)*
